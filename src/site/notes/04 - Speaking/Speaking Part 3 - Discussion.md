## üìã Format

- **Time:** ~4 minutes
- **Who:** You + Your partner (both candidates together)
- **Task:** Discuss options and agree on something
- **Visual:** Usually pictures or written options to discuss

## üéØ Typical Tasks

**Examples:**
- "Here are some activities for a school event. Discuss which would be best."
- "These are presents for a friend. Decide which two to buy."
- "Look at these ways to learn English. Which are most useful?"

**What you must do:**
1. Discuss ALL the options
2. Give your opinions
3. Listen to your partner
4. Agree on a decision/choice

---

## üí° Structure

### **1. Start the Discussion (30 sec)**
**Look at options together:**
- "Okay, let's look at these..."
- "So, we need to decide..."
- "Which one should we start with?"

### **2. Discuss Each Option (2-3 min)**
**For each option:**
- Give your opinion
- Ask partner's opinion
- Give reasons
- Agree or disagree politely

### **3. Make Decision (30 sec)**
**Reach agreement:**
- "So, shall we choose...?"
- "Do you agree that...?"
- "I think we both like... the best."

---

## üìù Example Task

**Task:** "You're organizing a class party. Here are some activities. Discuss which two would be best."

**Options:**
- Dancing
- Games
- Karaoke
- Watching a film
- Cooking together

---

**Example Dialogue:**

**You:** "Okay, let's look at these activities. What do you think about dancing?"

**Partner:** "I think dancing could be fun. Most people enjoy it. What's your opinion?"

**You:** "I agree, it's a good idea. What about games?"

**Partner:** "Games are fun too, but maybe not everyone likes the same games."

**You:** "That's true. How about karaoke?"

**Partner:** "I love karaoke! It's entertaining and everyone can join in. Do you like it?"

**You:** "Yes, I think it's great. But what about watching a film? That's quite passive."

**Partner:** "I agree, it might be a bit boring for a party. And cooking?"

**You:** "Cooking together could be interesting, but it might take too much time."

**Partner:** "Good point. So maybe dancing and karaoke?"

**You:** "Yes, I think those are the best two. Everyone can participate and they're both fun. Do you agree?"

**Partner:** "Absolutely! Let's choose dancing and karaoke."

## üîë Useful Language

### **Starting:**
- Shall we start with...?
- Let's look at...
- Okay, so we need to...
- Which one should we discuss first?

### **Giving Opinions:**
- I think...
- In my opinion...
- From my point of view...
- I'd say...
- Personally, I...

### **Asking for Opinions:**
- What do you think?
- Do you agree?
- How do you feel about...?
- What's your opinion on...?

### **Agreeing:**
- I agree
- Exactly / Absolutely
- That's true
- Good point
- I think so too

### **Disagreeing Politely:**
- I see what you mean, but...
- That's a good point, however...
- I'm not sure about that
- I see it differently
- Maybe, but...

### **Making Suggestions:**
- How about...?
- Why don't we...?
- We could...
- What if we...?
- Shall we...?

### **Comparing:**
- I think X is better than Y because...
- X is more... than Y
- Compared to X, Y is...
- Both are good, but...

### **Reaching Agreement:**
- So, shall we choose...?
- Do we both agree that...?
- I think we've decided on...
- So it's agreed - we'll go with...

## ‚úÖ Tips for Success

1. **Be cooperative:**
   - Work WITH your partner, not against them
   - Help if they're stuck

2. **Balance speaking time:**
   - Don't talk too much or too little
   - Aim for 50/50

3. **Show you're listening:**
   - Use eye contact
   - Nod, say "mm-hmm"
   - Respond to partner's ideas

4. **Use linking phrases:**
   - "As you said..."
   - "I agree with your point about..."
   - "Going back to what you mentioned..."

5. **Keep conversation natural:**
   - Don't sound rehearsed
   - React genuinely to partner

6. **Manage time:**
   - Don't spend too long on one option
   - Make sure you reach a decision
